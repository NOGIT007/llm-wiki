## What is Nano Banana 2?

Nano Banana 2 is a "reasoning-first" image model. Unlike older models that just guess what a chair looks like, Nano Banana 2 plans the scene. It understands coordinates, geography, and even the spelling of words before it renders a single pixel.

## The "Director's Method" Prompting Framework

Nano Banana 2 is a language model that generates images. Talk to it like a Creative Director, not a computer programmer.

Subject (Who/What) + Composition (Camera/Angle) + Action (Doing what) + Location (Where) + Style (Lighting/Film) + Format (Aspect Ratio/4K)

### Example: Professional Product Shot

Instead of just asking for "a coffee bag," use this:

**Prompt:** "A premium coffee bean bag labeled 'MIDNIGHT ROAST' in a bold serif font. 85mm macro lens, f/2.8. Sits on a dark oak table with steam rising from a cup next to it. Soft morning window light from the left. 16:9 aspect ratio, 4K resolution."

## The Ultimate Learning Hack: YouTube-to-Sketchnote

This is a two-step system that turns an hour-long video into a one-page "Sketchnote" summary in 60 seconds.

### Step 1: The Summarizer (Inside Gemini)

Paste your YouTube URL and ask:

"Analyze this video: **\[URL\]**. Summarize the core concepts into 5-7 direct, actionable steps. Keep it simple."

### Step 2: The Visualizer (Inside Gemini)

Once you have the summary, use this specific prompt to generate your learning asset:

"Visualize the summary of these notes. Create a realistic photograph of a dry-erase whiteboard with a light wooden frame. The content should be a hand-drawn sketchnote using 'graphic recording' style.\
\
• Layout: 9:16 vertical.\
• Colors: Black for outlines, Red and Blue for headers.\
• Structure: Title '**\[INSERT TITLE\]**' at the top. Five boxes with numbered doodles for each point.\
• Environment: Include a whiteboard eraser and markers resting on the bottom ledge."

## Pro Secrets for Success

- **The "Thinking Mode":** Gemini plans complex layouts before rendering. If you want a detailed chart, explicitly ask Gemini to "think through the layout first."
- **Text Distance Rule:** When adding text, specify the exact words and where they go (e.g., "Text 'GO' 20px above the button").
- **Multi-Turn Editing:** If the first image isn't perfect, just talk to Gemini. "Make the lighting warmer" or "Move the mountain to the right."
