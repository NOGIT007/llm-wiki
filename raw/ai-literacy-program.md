# AI Literacy Program

AI Literacy is the overarching learning framework that ties the General Track together. It starts with a foundation course that gives everyone a shared baseline, then branches into role-specific paths  with security, compliance, and ethics woven throughout rather than treated as afterthoughts.

The goal isn't just awareness. It's the confidence to use AI securely in your actual role, grounded in Visma's own policies and real-world AI threats.

Build real AI fluency across your organization — not just awareness, but the confidence to use AI securely in your actual role. Complete courses at your own pace through our training platform, starting with a foundation course and progressing through role-specific modules. Track your progress, earn certifications, and build verifiable AI competence. Every module is grounded in Visma's own policies and real-world OWASP AI threats, so learning translates directly into safer, smarter daily work.

## What You Get

### Foundation Course

A self-paced course on our learning portal covering what every Visma employee should know:

- Understanding AI capabilities and limitations
- General security awareness when working with AI tools
- Responsible AI use principles
- Based on existing AI Literacy Guide materials

**Strongly recommended for all employees.** Completion is tracked via our learning portal, and certification is issued upon finishing. This is the starting point — it gives you the context everything else builds on.

### Role-Specific Deep Dives

Self-paced training modules on our learning portal, developed in collaboration with Visma Security and Legal teams. Security and compliance topics are deepened for roles where it matters most. Each module includes role-specific scenarios and ends with a certification.

**Rollout priority:**

- **Priority 1: Leaders** — AI governance, risk management, team enablement decisions
- **Priority 2: Sales, HR, Finance, Customer Success, Project Management** — Role-tailored AI tools, data handling, audit implications
- **Priority 3: Marketing** — Content compliance, brand safety, GDPR considerations

### Weekly Blogs

At least one post per week covering:

- AI security news and threat landscape updates
- Policy and compliance announcements
- Practical tips for secure, responsible AI use
- Tool-specific security guidance

Published on the Emerging Technology Unit website, with regular guest contributions from Visma Security and Legal teams. Whether you're shipping AI-powered features or making tooling decisions, you get actionable insights to work confidently.

### Ethics

AI ethics is integrated into every learning path — not a standalone document nobody reads. You get:

- A dedicated module in the foundation course covering responsible AI principles
- Role-specific ethical scenarios in every deep-dive training module
- Practical frameworks for bias awareness, transparency, disclosure, and human accountability

AI ethics isn't a checkbox — it's a mindset. Practical ethical frameworks tailored to your role, so every team makes better decisions about how they build with and apply AI.

## How It Works

1. **Start with the foundation course** — self-paced on our learning portal, covers the essentials everyone needs
2. **Complete your role-specific training modules** — self-paced deep dives with content developed with Security and Legal teams
3. **Stay current** — weekly blogs, monthly AI Coffee Breaks, and our upcoming, later-announced add-ons for day-to-day questions
4. **Track your progress** — earn certifications and build verifiable AI competence through our learning portal

## Platform

All courses are self-paced on our learning portal, enabling:

- Self-paced course delivery
- Completion tracking per employee
- Certification and accomplishment issuance
- Reporting on organizational completion rates

## Who It's For

Every Visma employee. The foundation course is for everyone. Deep dives are tailored per role.

## Why It Matters

Generic AI training doesn't translate to safer, smarter daily work. AI Literacy is grounded in Visma's own policies and real-world OWASP AI threats, so what you learn maps directly to what you do.

Every module and every blog post connects back to the same framework — giving you one coherent program instead of scattered resources.

The EU AI Act has also put AI literacy formally on the agenda. Article 4 introduces expectations around AI knowledge for providers, deployers, and others involved in operating AI systems — essentially anyone in the AI value chain is expected to ensure their people have a sufficient level of understanding relative to what they work with. Whether and how that applies to your organization is for you to assess, but a structured program with tracked completions and verifiable certifications is naturally the kind of thing that speaks to those expectations.
