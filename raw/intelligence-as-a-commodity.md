**Intelligence is now a commodity. That changes everything.**

You already know the price of compute is collapsing. What you haven’t internalised is the price of intelligence. It’s free.

Not “cheap”. Not “discounted”. Free. The marginal cost of generating a coherent, context-aware response from a frontier model is now measured in fractions of a cent. That number will keep falling. Moore’s Law for AI isn’t about transistors. It’s about the cost of thought.

## The curve you’re not watching

Most people track model size or benchmark scores. Those are vanity metrics. The real signal is cost per token. In 2020, GPT-3 charged $0.06 per thousand tokens. Today, open-weight models deliver the same output for $0.0001. That’s a **99.8% drop in four years**.

*That’s not a price cut. That’s a phase transition.*

Cost curves this steep don’t stabilise. They keep falling until the product becomes ambient. Electricity didn’t stop at “affordable”. It became invisible. Intelligence is on the same trajectory.

## What commoditisation actually means

Commodities aren’t just cheap. They’re undifferentiated. When intelligence is free, the value moves up the stack.

1. **The model is the new compiler.** You don’t compete on how well you write assembly. You compete on what you build with it.
2. **Vertical movement is mandatory.** The winners will be the companies that embed intelligence into specific workflows, not the ones selling generic chatbots.
3. **Signal-to-noise becomes the bottleneck.** When everyone has access to the same raw intelligence, the scarce resource is the ability to filter, direct, and apply it.

*This isn’t about AI replacing jobs. It’s about AI replacing the idea of a job as a bundle of generic cognitive tasks.*

## The new surface area

The real estate that matters is no longer the model weights. It’s the agentic scaffolding around them.

- **Orchestration layers** that chain models, tools, and human oversight into repeatable pipelines.
- **Memory systems** that turn ephemeral context into persistent institutional knowledge.
- **Guardrails** that enforce policy, compliance, and brand voice without manual review.

These are the moats. The models themselves? They’re the new electricity. You don’t own the grid. You own the appliances.

## The hybrid workforce you’re not building

Most companies still treat AI as a tool. That’s like treating electricity as a candle. The future is a hybrid workforce where humans and agents collaborate in real time, each doing what they do best.

- **Humans** handle ambiguity, ethics, and creative leaps.
- **Agents** handle repetition, pattern matching, and cognitive offloading at scale.

The ratio isn’t 1:1. It’s 1:n. One human, many agents. The limiting factor isn’t the AI. It’s the human’s ability to delegate, verify, and integrate.

## Verifiability is the new currency

When intelligence is free, trust becomes expensive. The companies that win will be the ones that can prove their outputs are correct, compliant, and aligned.

- **Provenance tracking** for every decision, from raw data to final output.
- **Explainability layers** that don’t just show the answer but the reasoning path.
- **Audit trails** that let regulators, customers, and employees trace every step.

*This isn’t optional. It’s table stakes.*

## What you do next

1. **Stop treating AI as a feature.** It’s the substrate. Build on it, not with it.
2. **Move vertically.** Pick a workflow. Own it end-to-end. The model is just one component.
3. **Design for verifiability.** If you can’t prove it, you can’t scale it.
4. **Build agentic scaffolding.** The model is the engine. The scaffolding is the car.

Intelligence is no longer scarce. Attention is. The companies that win will be the ones that turn free intelligence into focused action.

**The commodity era isn’t coming. It’s here.**
