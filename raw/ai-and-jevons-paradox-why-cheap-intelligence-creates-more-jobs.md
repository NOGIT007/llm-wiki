> Artificial intelligence does not eliminate jobs by making cognitive work efficient; it makes intelligence cheaper, driving exponential new demand for human judgment and problem-solving.

## AI Drives the Cost of Cognitive Tasks Toward Zero

Algorithms routinely demonstrate the ability to write code, diagnose illnesses, and generate creative assets faster than humans. The immediate result is a drastic reduction in the cost of executing routine cognitive labor across all industries.

## Fear Centers on a Fixed-Demand Fallacy

The fear of mass unemployment rests on a flawed assumption: that the economy requires a fixed amount of code to be written or text to be edited. Under this logic, if a machine does the work of ten people, nine people lose their jobs. However, economic demand is not fixed; it is highly elastic.

## Efficiency Historically Increases Total Consumption

In 1865, engineers introduced steam engines that required far less coal to operate. Common sense dictated that national coal consumption would drop. Instead, total coal usage skyrocketed across the economy. 

## The Jevons Paradox Explains This Phenomenon

Economist William Stanley Jevons observed that increasing the efficiency of a resource does not lead to conservation. It lowers the cost of use, unlocking entirely new applications. Steam power became affordable for factories, trains, and ships, multiplying total demand.

## Intelligence and Labor Are the New Coal

AI applies the Jevons Paradox directly to cognitive work. As the cost of producing text, images, or code drops, organizations will not produce the same output with fewer people. They will produce exponentially more output.

## Cheaper Software Unlocks Infinite Niche Markets

Custom software was previously restricted to large organizations with massive budgets. As AI makes coding significantly more efficient, the cost of building applications plummets.
* Small businesses gain access to bespoke tools.
* Individual users can afford hyper-personalized micro-apps.
* Developers shift from writing syntax to architecting complex solutions.

## Creative Value Shifts from Execution to Direction

Photography did not destroy visual art; it democratized image-making. AI lowers the barrier to producing high-fidelity text and images in exactly the same way. The premium value now shifts from technical execution to creative direction and strategic idea generation.

## Professional Services Scale to Excluded Populations

When AI automates contract analysis or initial medical scans, lawyers and doctors do not disappear. Their services simply become more accessible.
* Cheaper diagnostics mean routine preventative scans for everyone, requiring more doctors to interpret results.
* Faster legal administration makes representation affordable for millions, creating massive new markets for legal guidance.

## Human Labor Moves Up the Value Chain

AI naturally absorbs repetitive drudge work like basic drafting and data sorting. Human labor transitions from manual execution to strategic oversight. When doing the work becomes cheap, deciding exactly what work to do becomes the most valuable skill in the market.

## Efficiency Solves Previously Unaffordable Problems

By drastically reducing the cost of intelligence, AI equips organizations to solve problems that were previously too expensive to address. Making our workflows more efficient ensures human judgment and strategic oversight remain in higher demand than ever before.
