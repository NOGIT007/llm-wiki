## How to Use Claude (2026 Edition): A Beginner's Guide to Your New AI Assistant

Updated: April 2, 2026

If you just got access to Claude, welcome! It might feel a bit like you are staring at a cockpit with too many buttons. Between "Artifacts," "Projects," and "Cowork," where do you even start? This guide breaks down the latest features as of April 2, 2026, so you can stop guessing and start doing.

## 1) The Chat: Your Starting Point

Think of the Chat as your "general office." It is where you go for quick questions, emails, or brainstorming.

▸ Memory: Claude now remembers your preferences across different chats. You do not have to repeat your job title or writing style every single time.

▸ Extended Thinking: For complex logic or math, Claude can "think" before responding, showing you its reasoning chain so you can verify its logic.

> Pro Tip
>
> Use Search grounded in real-time web data for the latest news or documentation.

## 2 ) Artifacts & Visuals: See Your Work

When you ask Claude to build a website, a budget tracker, or a chart, it opens a panel on the right side called an Artifact.

▸ Interactive Apps on Mobile: You can see these apps on your phone (iOS/Android) and interact with them — pulling up live charts or sketching diagrams directly in the conversation.

▸ Inline Visualizations: Claude can now generate charts, diagrams, and interactive visualizations directly inside your conversations, not just inside Artifacts. You don't need to do anything special for visual content to appear. Claude decides when a visual response is appropriate based on your question. But remember just ask naturally,

## 3) Cowork: Let Claude Use Your Computer

This is the "Digital Employee" mode. Cowork allows Claude to interact with your local files and — as of March 2026 — your desktop environment.

▸ Computer Use (Research Preview): Claude can now point, click, and navigate your screen. It can open applications that do not have an API, perform data entry across multiple systems, or run QA tests. Available on Pro and Max plans for macOS (Windows support coming soon).

▸ Dispatch: Start a task on your desktop (like a complex data scrape), then monitor or approve steps from your phone via a continuous conversation thread.

## 4 ) Projects: Your Specialized Folders

If you have a recurring task (like a weekly newsletter or a specific coding repo), create a Project.

▸ Internal Knowledge: Upload your company's style guide, API docs, and instructions. Every chat inside that project will automatically follow these rules.

▸ Organization-wide Sharing: For Team and Enterprise users, you can share Projects with your entire team (not for Cowork but it will come in a later version accordingly to Anthropic ), ensuring everyone uses the same context and standards.

## 5) Excel & PowerPoint Add-ins

Claude lives inside your spreadsheets and slide decks now, with a shared "short-term memory."

▸ Cross-App Context: Claude "talks" between Excel and PowerPoint. If you build a financial model in Excel, you can ask Claude to "create the summary slide for this" in PowerPoint without re-explaining the data.

▸ Availability: The Excel and PowerPoint add-ins are available on paid Claude plans. In the Desktop go to "Get apps and extension" in the left corner.

## 6) Comparison: When to Use Which Mode?

The biggest difference between these modes is how Memory and Context are handled. Use this table to decide where to start your next task.

| Feature | Standard Chat | Project (in Chat) | Cowork (Folder-based) | Cowork Project |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary Goal | Quick Q&A, brainstorming. | Ongoing work with specific docs. | Autonomous file/task execution. | Repeated local automation workflows. |
| Memory Scope | Global. Learns your general bio/style across all chats. | Project-Specific. Memory is siloed to just this project. | Session-based. Uses CLAUDE.md project instructions and auto-memory that persists across sessions. | Local Project. Remembers context of previous tasks in that folder via CLAUDE.md and auto-memory. |
| Knowledge | Training data + Search. | Your uploaded files (Project Knowledge). | Your local folder + Computer screen. | Local folder + Persistent instructions. |
| Output Type | Text, code, and Artifacts. | Refined text based on your docs. | Direct File Action (Edits .xlsx, .pptx). | Scheduled, recurring file tasks. |
| Best For... | "Write an email to my boss." | "Draft a blog post using our 2026 Brand Guide." | "Organize my Downloads folder by date." | "Every Friday, audit this folder and create a summary PPT." |

## 7) Plugins & Skills: Adding "Superpowers"

▸ Plugins: One-click toolkits (like the Marketing or Legal pack) that add commands like `/draft-post` or `/security-review`. Browse available plugins at [claude.com/plugins](https://claude.com/plugins).

▸ Skills: Procedural "expert packs." Unlike Projects (which hold files), Skills teach Claude *how* to do something. You can browse the Customize section in Claude Desktop to install partner-built skills.

▸ Resources & Use Cases: To find the best plugins and workflows for your specific industry, explore the official [Claude Resources & Use Cases](https://claude.com/resources/use-cases).

## Quick Start Checklist for Today

- Enable Memory in your settings so Claude gets to know your role.
- Explore [Use Cases ](https://claude.com/resources/use-cases)and browse [Plugins ](https://claude.com/plugins)to find industry-specific tools.
- Create a Project for your most repetitive work task (e.g., "Weekly Reporting").
- Install the Excel add-in via "Get Add-ins" to start working across your files.
- Try a visual: Ask Claude to "Create an interactive chart of my team's velocity" to see the new visualization engine.
