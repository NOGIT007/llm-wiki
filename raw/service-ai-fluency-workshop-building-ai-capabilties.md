# AI Fluency Workshop

Your teams have AI tools. The question is whether everyone knows how to communicate with them, what to delegate, and how to check what comes back.

This workshop gives your leadership team a shared language for AI decisions — so departments stop reinventing their own approaches and your organization moves from scattered experimentation to coordinated use.

**This is for teams where:**

- Multiple departments are experimenting independently with no shared criteria
- Executives range from AI-enthusiastic to AI-skeptical, with no structured forum to reconcile those positions
- The next step requires coordinated investment, not more pilots

---

## The Approach

### The 4D Framework

At the core of the workshop is the 4D Framework — four questions every team should be able to answer about any AI task:

- **Delegate** — What do we hand to AI, and what do we keep?
- **Describe** — How do we communicate what we need?
- **Discern** — How do we evaluate what comes back?
- **Declare** — Who owns the result and takes responsibility?

When everyone in your organization can answer these four questions consistently, you move from fragmented experimentation to coordinated capability.

### Organizational Capabilities

Individual fluency is necessary but not sufficient. The workshop also maps the organizational capabilities that determine whether AI investment translates into business results:

- A clear, communicated AI stance (the three-bucket framework: prohibited, permitted, allowed)
- Healthy data ecosystems that AI can actually work with
- Version control and change management practices
- User-centric focus in AI deployment

Without these in place, individual productivity gains get absorbed by organizational friction. The workshop helps your team assess where you stand and what to prioritize.
