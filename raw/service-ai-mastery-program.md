### From Understanding to Practical Use: Building AI Capability in Your Team

After completing the AI Fluency Workshop, your team now has a common way to discuss and evaluate AI-related decisions. However, knowing the principles of a framework is not the same as applying them effectively in real work. To bridge this gap, teams need structured practice—opportunities to use these principles in their actual tasks, with guidance on how to refine their approach over time.

The AI Mastery Program is designed to provide this practice. It builds on the **4D Framework** (Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence) introduced in the workshop, but shifts the focus from theory to application. The program is structured around five levels of progression, each representing a deeper level of competence:

1. **Explorer**: Learning to apply the 4D Framework to simple, low-risk tasks.
2. **Practitioner**: Using the framework for more complex work, with peer feedback.
3. **Guide**: Leading small group exercises and helping others apply the framework.
4. **Advisor**: Evaluating AI decisions at a team or project level, identifying risks and improvements.
5. **Master**: Training others, refining the framework for your organization’s specific needs, and ensuring long-term capability.

Each level includes exercises based on your team’s real work—not hypothetical scenarios. This ensures that the skills developed are directly relevant to your daily tasks. Progress is validated through peer review, where team members assess each other’s work against clear criteria. This approach helps maintain quality while fostering a shared understanding of what good AI decision-making looks like.

By the end of the program, your team will have:
- A group of individuals capable of training others, reducing reliance on external support.
- A repeatable process for evaluating and improving AI-related decisions.
- Confidence in applying the 4D Framework to new challenges as they arise.

The program is delivered digitally and can be completed at your team’s own pace. Most participants finish within 2 to 6 weeks, depending on how much time they dedicate to the exercises each week. There are no fixed deadlines, allowing teams to balance the program with their existing workload.
