# Intelligence as a Commodity

The cost of AI intelligence has undergone a phase transition. Between 2020 and 2024, the price per thousand tokens dropped from $0.06 (GPT-3) to $0.0001 on open-weight models — a **99.8% reduction**. This trajectory mirrors how electricity moved from expensive to invisible, and intelligence is following the same path. See [[Intelligence Commoditization]] and [[Jevons Paradox]] for broader implications.

When intelligence becomes a commodity, it is no longer a differentiator. The value shifts up the stack. Models become "the new compiler" — you compete on what you build with them, not on the models themselves. This demands vertical integration: embedding intelligence into specific workflows rather than selling generic chatbots.

The article identifies three new moats: **orchestration layers** that chain models, tools, and human oversight into pipelines; **memory systems** that turn ephemeral context into institutional knowledge; and **guardrails** that enforce compliance without manual review. These constitute the [[AI Agents]] scaffolding that matters more than raw model weights.

The future workforce operates at a 1:n ratio — one human directing many agents. Humans handle ambiguity, ethics, and creative leaps while agents handle repetition and pattern matching at scale. The limiting factor is the human's ability to delegate and verify. This connects directly to [[Verification]] as "the new currency": provenance tracking, explainability layers, and audit trails become table stakes when trust is more expensive than intelligence itself.

The core directive: stop treating AI as a feature, move vertically, design for verifiability, and build agentic scaffolding.
