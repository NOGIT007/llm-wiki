## Summary

AI does not just help startups -- it rewrites their organizational DNA. This article argues that AI dismantles traditional org charts and rebuilds them around velocity, judgment, and [[Work Structure]] that treats humans as editors rather than executors.

## Key Claims

- **The first 10 hires are obsolete** -- A solo founder with an LLM can generate 100+ ad variations per hour, spin up landing pages in 30 minutes, and handle first-line support 24/7. This is not efficiency; it is structural collapse of traditional team roles.

- **Optimize for velocity, not headcount** -- The strongest startups optimize for shipping speed, creative iteration, and decision velocity. The bottleneck is no longer talent but judgment: AI generates too many options, so choosing what to do becomes the critical skill.

- **Own outcomes, not functions** -- The old model (marketer, designer, engineer each owning a function) gives way to integrated individuals who own outcomes. The org chart flattens because the work itself has changed.

- **The hybrid workforce** -- Humans move up the stack. Junior developers become prompt engineers, data analysts become signal curators, customer support becomes AI oversight. The best hires are those who spot when AI is wrong ([[Verification]]).

- **Specialists win over generalists** -- AI makes everyone fast and scrappy, so the advantage shifts to vertical depth. The moat is "agentic scaffolding" where AI handles the predictable and humans handle the unpredictable.

- **Leadership becomes system design** -- Founders manage AI-human feedback loops, not people. The key question shifts from "How do we scale our team?" to "How do we scale our AI's judgment?"
