# AI Mastery Program

A five-level progression program by [[Morten Andre Nilsson]] that bridges the gap between understanding the [[4D Framework]] in theory and applying it in real work. It is designed as the next step after the AI Fluency Workshop and directly addresses [[The 201 Gap]] — the missing middle layer in corporate [[AI Adoption]].

## Five Levels of Progression

The program structures competence development through increasingly deep engagement with the 4D Framework (Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence):

1. **Explorer** — Apply the framework to simple, low-risk tasks
2. **Practitioner** — Handle complex work with peer feedback
3. **Guide** — Lead small group exercises and help others apply the framework
4. **Advisor** — Evaluate AI decisions at team or project level, identifying risks and improvements
5. **Master** — Train others, refine the framework for organizational needs, ensure long-term capability

## Design Principles

Every level uses exercises drawn from participants' **real work** rather than hypothetical scenarios. Progress is validated through **peer review** against clear criteria, building shared understanding of what good AI decision-making looks like. This peer certification model creates internal multipliers who can train others, reducing reliance on external support.

## Delivery and Outcomes

The program is self-paced and delivered digitally, with most participants finishing in 2-6 weeks. By completion, organizations gain: a group of internal trainers, a repeatable process for evaluating AI decisions, and confidence applying the [[4D Framework]] to new challenges. The ultimate goal is **self-sustaining AI capability** — the organization no longer depends on external vendors for [[AI Adoption]] competence. This connects to the broader challenge of [[Work Structure]] in AI-augmented teams.
