## Overview

NotebookLM is [[Google]]'s document analysis tool that lets users upload sources and interact with them through AI-powered Q&A, summaries, and structured extraction. It is one of the most frequently referenced tools in the corpus, appearing across five sources.

## Key Features

- **Research Workflows** -- Structured processes for ingesting, querying, and synthesizing information from uploaded documents.
- **Data Grid Extraction** -- Tabular extraction capability that pulls structured data from unstructured sources into grid format.
- **Tool Chains with AntiGravity** -- Integration with the AntiGravity platform to create multi-step research pipelines that chain NotebookLM outputs into downstream workflows.
- **Studio Tools** -- Built-in utilities for audio overviews, study guides, and other derivative outputs.

## Role in the Corpus

NotebookLM is discussed both as a standalone research tool and as a component in larger [[Work Structure]] pipelines. It also appears in the [[Hallucinations]] discussion as a tool that grounds AI responses in uploaded source material, reducing fabrication risk.
