# Intelligence Commoditization

Intelligence — the ability to analyze, synthesize, generate, and reason — is becoming a cheap, abundant commodity. The cost of an LLM API call dropped 99.8% in roughly four years. This is not incremental improvement; it is a phase transition.

## What Changes

When intelligence is cheap:

- **Models become like compilers.** Essential infrastructure, but not where competitive advantage lives. No one brags about their C compiler; soon, no one will brag about which LLM they use.
- **Value moves to orchestration.** The differentiator becomes how you combine models, memory, guardrails, and tools — not the raw model itself.
- **Memory and context become moats.** A model is generic; a model with your company's institutional knowledge is specific and valuable.
- **Verifiability replaces cost as the binding constraint.** When generation is nearly free, the bottleneck is knowing whether the output is correct. See [[Verification]].

## The Displacement Pattern

As intelligence commoditizes, the skills that remain valuable are precisely those that are hard to commoditize: judgment, taste, relationship context, and the ability to ask the right questions. This mirrors every previous commoditization wave — when materials got cheap, design became valuable; when distribution got cheap, content became valuable.

## Jevons Connection

Cheap intelligence triggers [[Jevons Paradox]]: total demand for cognitive work increases, even as the per-unit cost collapses.
