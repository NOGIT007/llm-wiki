# The 201 Gap

Corporate AI training has a hole in the middle. Organizations teach **101-level** tool tours ("here's ChatGPT, here's a prompt template") and **401-level** technical deep dives (APIs, RAG, vector databases). The missing **201 layer** — judgment, integration, and reliable deployment — is where the workforce actually lives and where [[AI Adoption]] fails.

## The Evidence

80% of organizations have explored ChatGPT and Copilot, but only **5% reached production**. Gartner projected 30% of generative AI initiatives would be abandoned after proof-of-concept by end of 2025. The technology works; the training does not.

## The Jagged Frontier

BCG and Harvard studied 758 consultants using AI. Inside AI's capability boundary, users achieved 40% higher quality and 25% faster completion. **Outside that boundary, performance dropped 19 points below not using AI at all.** This is the [[Jagged Frontier]] — the critical skill is not using AI but knowing where the boundary is. Nobody teaches that.

## Six Missing Skills

The article identifies six capabilities absent from most [[AI Adoption]]: (1) **Context Assembly** — information architecture for non-human readers, (2) **Quality Judgment** — distinguishing good output from confident [[Hallucinations]], (3) **Task Decomposition** — identifying which 30% of work AI handles well, (4) **Iterative Refinement** — building on partial results across rounds, (5) **Workflow Integration** — embedding AI without creating bottlenecks, and (6) **Frontier Recognition** — mapping where AI performs reliably in your specific domain.

## The Fix

Training programs fail because they treat AI as a tool to learn rather than a capability to develop. Organizations that close the gap create safe failure environments with real work, document the frontier specific to their business, and preserve apprenticeship for developing the professional judgment AI cannot replace. This is fundamentally a management problem, not a technology problem. The [[4D Framework]] and structured [[Work Structure]] approaches offer practical paths forward.
