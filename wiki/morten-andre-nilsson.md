## Overview

Morten Andre Nilsson is an author and practitioner whose voice appears across multiple sources in the corpus. He focuses on AI judgment, capability building, and structured approaches to training humans and AI agents for effective collaboration.

## Key Contributions

- **DO-MAKE-KNOW Framework** -- A three-layer model for AI capability development: *Do* (task execution), *Make* (artifact creation), and *Know* (knowledge internalization). Positions AI fluency as a progression, not a single skill.
- **4D Framework** -- A structured methodology for designing AI workflows, referenced in the training and mastery program sources.
- **Co-worker Approach** -- Treats [[Claude]] not as a tool to prompt but as a collaborator to train. Emphasizes questioning, iterative refinement, and building shared context over time rather than one-shot prompting.

## Role in the Corpus

Nilsson's frameworks bridge the gap between [[Prompt Engineering]] technique and organizational adoption. His training programs ([[src-ai-fluency-workshop]], [[src-ai-mastery-program]]) operationalize the theoretical concepts found in other sources.
