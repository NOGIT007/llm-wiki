# Work Structure

How you decompose a task determines whether AI can help with it. This is the central structural insight connecting the [[Jagged Frontier]], [[Verification]], and [[AI Agents]] concepts.

## The Core Principle

A task's "AI-suitability" is not fixed — it depends on how you break it down. Two dimensions matter:

- **Decomposable** — Can the task be split into independent sub-tasks? If yes, each piece can be routed to the right tool (human or AI) based on where the frontier falls.
- **Verifiable** — Can each sub-task's output be checked cheaply? If yes, AI errors get caught before they propagate.

Tasks that are both decomposable and verifiable are ideal AI candidates. Tasks that are neither should stay fully human — for now.

## Meta-Skill Over Domain Expertise

The ability to see work as a structure of sub-tasks — and to judge which sub-tasks sit inside the [[Jagged Frontier]] — is more valuable than deep domain expertise in a single area. A mediocre coder who decomposes well outperforms a great coder who prompts monolithically.

## Impact on Organizations

This reframes AI adoption from "which roles get automated?" to "which task structures benefit from AI augmentation?" The answer changes how teams are organized, how projects are scoped, and how quality is managed.
