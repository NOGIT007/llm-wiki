# AI Adoption

Patterns and programs for rolling out AI capabilities across an organization. The challenge is not technical — it is behavioral, cultural, and structural.

## The Workload Paradox

The central irony of AI adoption: efficiency gains tend to expand work rather than reduce it. Teams that adopt AI don't finish at 3 PM — they take on more projects, raise quality bars, and discover tasks that weren't feasible before. This is [[Jevons Paradox]] applied to knowledge work.

## Program Ladder

Effective adoption follows a progression:

- **Coffee breaks** — Low-commitment, curiosity-driven introductions. "Come see what this can do." No homework, no pressure.
- **Literacy programs** — Structured awareness: what AI can and cannot do, security basics, responsible use policies.
- **Fluency workshops** — Hands-on skill building with real work tasks. Participants leave with workflows they actually use.
- **Mastery programs** — Deep integration: building agents, designing tool chains, training others. Creates internal champions.

## Key Challenge

Most organizations stall between literacy and fluency — they understand AI conceptually but lack the [[the-201-gap|201-level skills]] to integrate it into daily work. Bridging this gap requires practice with real tasks, not more slide decks.

## Success Pattern

The organizations that adopt AI well treat it as a management change, not a technology deployment. New tools require new workflows, new review processes, and new definitions of "done."
