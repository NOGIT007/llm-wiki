# AI Fluency Workshop – Building AI Capabilities

A half-day or full-day workshop by [[Morten Andre Nilsson]] designed for leadership teams in mid-to-large organizations. The goal: give departments a **shared language** for AI decisions so the organization moves from scattered experimentation to coordinated use.

## The 4D Framework

At the core of the workshop sits the [[4D Framework]] — four questions every team should answer about any AI task:

- **Delegate** — What do we hand to AI, and what do we keep?
- **Describe** — How do we communicate what we need?
- **Discern** — How do we evaluate what comes back?
- **Declare** — Who owns the result and takes responsibility?

When everyone answers these four questions consistently, fragmented experimentation becomes coordinated capability. This directly addresses the judgment gap described in [[The 201 Gap]] — the missing middle layer between basic tool literacy and technical deep dives.

## Organizational Capabilities

Individual fluency is necessary but not sufficient. The workshop maps organizational prerequisites for [[AI Adoption]]: a clear AI stance using a three-bucket framework (prohibited, permitted, allowed), healthy data ecosystems, version control and change management practices, and user-centric deployment focus. Without these, individual productivity gains get absorbed by organizational friction.

## Format and Outcomes

Delivered on-site or remotely in half-day (two focused sessions) or full-day formats. Both cover the [[4D Framework]], organizational capabilities assessment, and hands-on work with participants' own use cases. Participants leave with a shared vocabulary for AI decisions, clear delegation criteria, leadership alignment on AI governance, and the 4D Framework as a practical tool for immediate use. The workshop feeds into the [[AI Training Programs]] progression described in the companion AI Mastery Program.
