## Summary

Drawing on Jouni Heikniemi's analysis, this article identifies six critical [[AI Security]] areas that every organization must address in 2026. The core message: don't wait for perfect security -- start small, classify risks, and evolve governance iteratively.

## The Six Realities

1. **Risk Classification** -- Not all AI carries the same risk. A fraud detection system (predictable, testable) is fundamentally different from a GenAI assistant (creative, unpredictable). Calibrate controls to the actual risk profile or people will work around overly strict rules.

2. **Shadow AI** -- Employees already bring personal AI habits into the workplace. Hard bans fail. The real work is cultural: building open dialogue about what tools people actually use, making this a talent management question as much as a security one.

3. **Prompt Injection** -- An unsolved fundamental problem. LLMs cannot reliably distinguish data from instructions. Any system processing external content is vulnerable. Mitigation requires layered defenses and never connecting external-facing AI directly to critical systems.

4. **[[AI Agents]] as Digital Insiders** -- Agents with system access become insiders that follow the letter of permissions, not the spirit. Governance frameworks designed for human-scale actions are insufficient. Agent monitoring must resemble human activity monitoring, not software monitoring.

5. **AI-Driven Fraud** -- Even organizations avoiding AI face AI-powered threats: deepfakes, receipt forgery, hyper-personalized phishing. Threat modeling must extend beyond your perimeter to your entire trust network.

6. **New Fundamental Business Risks** -- [[Hallucinations]] carry legal liability (companies are responsible for AI promises, even hallucinated ones). The EU AI Act requires formalized governance. AI-amplified human actions can cause massive damage before anyone notices.

## Key Takeaway

Organizations that thrive will build open dialogue, classify risks honestly, and evolve governance continuously. Perfect security is unattainable; good-enough security built iteratively is achievable.
