# The 201 Gap

A missing middle layer in how organizations teach AI. The landscape has plenty of 101-level content (tool tours, "here's how to use ChatGPT") and 401-level content (fine-tuning, RLHF, model architecture). But the 201 level — practical judgment, integration, and deployment — is largely absent.

## Six Missing Skills

1. **Context assembly** — Knowing what information the model needs and how to provide it. Not just "write a good prompt" but curating the right inputs.
2. **Quality judgment** — Recognizing when AI output is good enough versus subtly wrong. This requires domain knowledge plus AI awareness.
3. **Task decomposition** — Breaking work into AI-suitable and human-suitable pieces. The structural skill behind [[Work Structure]].
4. **Iterative refinement** — Treating AI output as a first draft, not a final product. Knowing when and how to push for better results.
5. **Workflow integration** — Embedding AI into existing processes without breaking what already works. Change management, not just technology.
6. **Frontier recognition** — Sensing where the [[Jagged Frontier]] falls for a specific task. The meta-skill that prevents the 19-point penalty from working outside the boundary.

## Why It Matters

Without 201-level skills, organizations oscillate between naive enthusiasm (everything to AI) and blanket skepticism (nothing to AI). The middle ground — informed, selective, verified use — requires training that barely exists.
