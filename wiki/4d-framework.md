# 4D Framework

A four-step decision framework for organizational AI use: **Delegate, Describe, Discern, Declare.** Designed to give teams a shared vocabulary for making AI-related decisions across departments.

## The Four Ds

1. **Delegate** — Identify which tasks (or sub-tasks) can be handed to AI. Not "should we use AI?" but "which specific pieces of this work are AI-suitable?" Requires understanding [[Work Structure]] and the [[Jagged Frontier]].

2. **Describe** — Articulate the task clearly enough for an AI to execute it. This is prompt engineering at the organizational level: defining inputs, expected outputs, constraints, and success criteria. Vague delegation produces vague results.

3. **Discern** — Evaluate the AI's output. Is it good enough? Where did it fall short? This is the [[Verification]] step, and it requires the quality judgment skills identified in [[the-201-gap|The 201 Gap]].

4. **Declare** — Make an explicit decision: accept, reject, or refine. Then communicate that decision. This closes the loop and creates organizational learning — teams build shared intuition for what works.

## Why a Framework Matters

Without shared language, AI adoption becomes ad hoc. One team over-trusts, another refuses to engage. The 4D framework doesn't prescribe answers — it prescribes a consistent process for arriving at them, making AI decisions discussable across roles and departments.
